# User Persona: Ravi

## Background
* **Name**: Ravi
* **Age**: 14
* **Class**: 9th Grade
* **School**: Government High School (State Board)
* **Location**: Semi-urban / rural-adjacent town

## Attributes & Device Access
* **Primary Device**: Shared family Android smartphone (budget level, 5.5" screen, occasional lag) and school computer lab (desktop running Windows 7/10, limited internet speed).
* **Language Profile**: Learns in a mix of the regional language and English (Bilingual textbook). Needs visual clarity and simple English explanations.
* **Learning Style**: Tactile and visual learner. Quickly loses interest with heavy pages of text. Likes games and challenges.

## Goals & Objectives
* **Pass exams with high marks**: Wants to score well in the Environmental Science and Physics components of Science.
* **Visualize invisible processes**: Wants to actually *see* what air pressure, wind currents, and air quality indexes look like rather than just memorizing definitions like "Wind is moving air."
* **Understand local context**: Wants to know why the factory outside his village makes the air feel heavy on winter mornings and how cyclones (which hit the nearby coastline) actually spin.

## Pain Points & Barriers
* **Abstract Textbook Diagrams**: Static 2D monochrome diagrams in state textbooks fail to explain dynamic topics (like the Coriolis effect, temperature changes, or pollution spreading).
* **Rote Memorization Focus**: Traditional classroom settings force him to memorize formulas and facts without understanding the underlying science.
* **Technological Constraints**: Modern simulation tools often require heavy downloads, high-end PCs, or complex logins, which fail on his phone or the slow school computers.
* **Lack of Instant Feedback**: In class, he doesn't know if his mental model is correct until a test grade returns weeks later.

## How Atmosphere Explorer Helps Ravi
1. **Interactive Visualization**: He can turn on the "Science Layer" to see invisible pressure fronts and wind vector arrows.
2. **Instant Cause-and-Effect**: Sliding the wind direction or speed slider immediately bends trees, blows smoke in different directions, and shifts the AQI meter in real-time.
3. **Low-friction Performance**: Built with pure HTML5 Canvas/SVG, loading instantly on old browsers and budget mobile devices without lag.
4. **Gamified Missions**: The structured challenges provide immediate goals (e.g., "Keep the AQI below 50") with instant rewards (stars) and contextual hints when he gets stuck.
