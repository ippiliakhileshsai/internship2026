# Requirement Gathering — States of Matter Explorer

---

## Functional Requirements
What should the simulation do?

Students should be able to:
✅ Select any of the 4 states of matter — Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma
✅ Adjust temperature (-200°C to 6000°C) and observe particle behavior change
✅ Adjust pressure (Vacuum to Extreme) and see its effect on particles
✅ Watch automatic phase transitions at correct temperatures
✅ Control particle count (10–120) for density visualization
✅ Search any substance and see its real melting and boiling points
✅ See special multi-stage behavior (e.g. Sea Water evaporating to Salt)
✅ Play, Pause, Reset, and Randomize the simulation
✅ Take a quiz to test their knowledge
✅ Switch the interface language (English, Hindi, Telugu)

---

## Non-Functional Requirements
How should it feel?

✅ Easy to use — intuitive sliders and clearly labeled controls
✅ Visually engaging — glowing particles, smooth animations, color-coded states
✅ Works in low internet — single HTML file, no backend required
✅ Fast loading — no frameworks, no dependencies except Google Fonts
✅ Scientifically accurate — real melting and boiling point data for all substances
✅ Multilingual — accessible to Hindi and Telugu speaking students

---

## Learning Requirements
What learning outcome should happen?

After using the simulation, students should be able to:
✅ Describe the particle arrangement and motion in each state of matter
✅ Explain how temperature and pressure affect the state of a substance
✅ Identify the correct state of a substance at any given temperature
✅ Predict what happens during phase transitions (melting, boiling, sublimation)
✅ Differentiate between evaporation and boiling
✅ Recall real-world examples of solids, liquids, gases, and plasma
✅ Understand special cases like dry ice sublimation and sea water evaporation
