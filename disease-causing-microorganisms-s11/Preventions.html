Prevention of Diseases Caused by Microorganisms

Learning simple prevention methods can help us stay healthy and stop infections from spreading.

Why Prevention is Important

Microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa can cause many diseases. Prevention is important because it helps protect individuals and communities before illness begins. Good habits reduce the spread of infection and support a healthier environment for everyone.

Personal Hygiene

Personal hygiene helps prevent germs from entering the body and spreading from one person to another.

  • Wash hands regularly with soap, especially before eating and after using the toilet.
  • Cover your mouth and nose while coughing or sneezing.
  • Drink clean water and eat safe, properly cooked food.
  • Keep your body, clothes, and surroundings clean.

Health Awareness Tips

  • Wash hands often and avoid touching your face with dirty hands.
  • Use clean drinking water and wash fruits and vegetables before eating.
  • Take vaccines on time according to medical advice.
  • Keep classrooms, homes, and public places clean.
  • Do not allow water to collect in open containers because mosquitoes can breed there.
  • Use a handkerchief, tissue, or your elbow when coughing or sneezing.

Conclusion

Prevention is better than cure. By following personal hygiene, taking vaccinations, and maintaining sanitation, we can reduce the spread of diseases caused by microorganisms. Small healthy habits practiced every day can make a big difference in keeping us safe.
