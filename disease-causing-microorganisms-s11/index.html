What are they?

Disease-causing microorganisms are tiny living things that can make us sick

Some microorganisms are helpful, but others can cause disease when they enter the body, grow quickly, or release toxins. You need a microscope to study most of them.

It is a virus

Viruses are not the same as bacteria. They need living cells to make more copies of themselves.

It can feel different

Some people feel only a little sick, while others need much more rest and care.

We can help stop it

Smart habits like handwashing, fresh air, and staying home when sick can slow the spread.

Microorganisms

Different microorganisms can cause different diseases

Microorganisms are tiny living or almost-living things that can only be seen with special tools. Some cause disease, and some are helpful or harmless.

Bacteria

Some bacteria are helpful, but others can cause infections like strep throat.

Viruses

Viruses are tiny invaders. Some cause illnesses like flu, measles, or chickenpox.

Fungi

Fungi can live in damp places and may cause skin problems like ringworm.

Protozoa

Protozoa are one-celled organisms that can sometimes spread through water or insects.

Kid clue

If it needs living cells to copy itself, that is a big clue it is a virus.

How it spreads

Microorganisms can spread in air, water, food, and through contact

When people talk, cough, sneeze, share food, touch surfaces, or drink unsafe water, germs can move from one place to another. Good ventilation and smart hygiene help.

Air droplets

Some droplets fall quickly, and some stay floating longer in stuffy spaces.

Close contact

It is easier for germs to move between people when they are face-to-face nearby.

Shared spaces

Open windows or fans can help bring in fresh air and move old air out.

Prevention

Simple actions make a superhero-sized difference

These habits do not need capes, but they do help protect classrooms, homes, and teams.

1

Wash hands

Scrub with soap for about 20 seconds, especially before eating and after coughing.

2

Cover coughs

Use your elbow or a tissue so droplets do not fly out as far.

3

Let fresh air in

Open windows when possible or spend time outdoors if everyone is feeling well.

4

Rest when sick

Staying home when you feel ill helps your body heal and protects other people.
