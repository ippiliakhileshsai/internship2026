🌍 Fossil Fuels: A Journey of Millions of Years!

Fossil fuels (Coal, Oil, and Natural Gas) are created from ancient plants and sea creatures buried deep underground over millions of years. Because humans burn them infinitely faster than they naturally form, they are non-renewable! Explore this simulation to see how they form, how we use them, their impact on the climate, and how we can restore balance.

1. Ancient Organisms

Life in Prehistoric Oceans

Millions of years ago, the Earth was covered in warm shallow seas. Tiny sea organisms (plankton) and ancient plants lived, captured energy from the sun, and died. They sank to the muddy ocean floor, forming the base of what would become fossil fuels.

💡
Fun Discovery Fact:

Did you know? Most oil and natural gas began as tiny sea creatures smaller than a pinhead!
