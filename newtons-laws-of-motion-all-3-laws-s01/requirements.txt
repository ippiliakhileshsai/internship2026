Functional Requirements

What should the simulation do?

• Apply force to an object

• Increase or decrease the mass of an object

• Observe object acceleration in real time

• Compare motion under different forces

• Show the effects of balanced and unbalanced forces

• Demonstrate action-reaction force pairs

• Allow users to reset and rerun experiments

• Display numerical values of:
• Force (N)
• Mass (kg)
• Acceleration (m/s²)

• Provide feedback for incorrect predictions

Non-Functional Requirements

How should it feel?

• Easy to use for school students

• Mobile-friendly

• Smooth performance on low-end devices

• Fast loading time

• Interactive and engaging

• Attractive animations and visuals

• Clear labels and instructions

• Beginner-friendly and accessible

Learning Requirements

After using the simulation, students should be able to:

Newton's First Law

• Explain the concept of inertia

• Distinguish between motion and rest

• Predict what happens when no external force acts on an object

Newton's Second Law

• Understand the relationship between force, mass, and acceleration

• Calculate the effects of changing force or mass

• Predict acceleration under different conditions

Newton's Third Law

• Identify action-reaction force pairs

• Explain why forces always occur in pairs

• Apply the concept to real-world examples such as:
• Walking
• Jumping
• Rocket launches
