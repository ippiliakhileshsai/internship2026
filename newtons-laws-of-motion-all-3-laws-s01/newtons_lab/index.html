👤 Who Was Isaac Newton?

The genius who changed how we understand the universe! 🌍

🧑‍🔬
Sir Isaac Newton
4 Jan 1643 – 31 Mar 1727
🔢 Mathematician ⚛️ Physicist 🔭 Astronomer
" If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants. "
📅 Newton's Life Timeline
1643
Born in Woolsthorpe, England — three months after his father died. He was so tiny at birth that nobody thought he'd survive! 👶
1661
Joined Cambridge University at age 18. He worked as a servant to pay for his studies. 📚
1666
The "Miracle Year" — Cambridge closed due to plague. Newton went home and discovered gravity, calculus, and the nature of light — all in one year! 🍎
1687
Published Principia Mathematica — the most important physics book ever written. It introduced the 3 Laws of Motion! 📜
1703
Became President of the Royal Society — the world's most prestigious science club. 🏛️
1727
Died peacefully at age 84. Buried in Westminster Abbey with kings and queens! ⭐
🍎
The Apple Story
Legend says a falling apple gave Newton the idea for gravity. While the apple may be real, the insight took years of hard thinking — not just one moment!
🌈
Rainbow Secrets
Newton used a prism to show that white light is actually made of ALL the colours of the rainbow. He invented the colour spectrum!
🔭
Built a Telescope
Frustrated with regular telescopes, Newton designed and built a brand-new type — the reflecting telescope — that is still used by astronomers today!
🔢
Invented Calculus
Newton invented calculus (advanced maths used for rockets, computers, and phones) at age 23. Leibniz invented it independently at the same time — big argument followed!
🏠
Lonely Childhood
When Newton was 3, his mother remarried and left him with his grandmother. He was so angry he once threatened to burn the house down!
🐱
The Cat Flap
Newton is said to have invented the cat flap — a small hole in the door for his cat and her kittens to pass through freely. Useful science! 😄
📜
Principia Mathematica (1687)
Newton's masterpiece took him 18 months of intense writing. It described gravity, planetary orbits, the tides, and the 3 Laws of Motion — all using mathematics. It is considered the greatest scientific book ever published!
