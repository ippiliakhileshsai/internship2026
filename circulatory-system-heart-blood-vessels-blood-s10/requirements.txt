CIRCULATORY SYSTEM (HEART, BLOOD VESSELS, BLOOD) SIMULATION

PROJECT REQUIREMENTS :

---> We have collected resources from NCERT textbooks, educational websites, and biology reference materials. We also used ChatGPT for content organization and explanations.

Resources:

* https://ncert.nic.in/
* https://byjus.com/biology/circulatory-system/
* https://www.britannica.com/science/circulatory-system
* https://kidshealth.org/
* https://www.biologyonline.com/

---> Develop an educational simulation on the Human Circulatory System.
---> Provide interactive learning for students of Classes 6–10.
---> Use simple language and engaging visuals.
---> Include real-life examples related to heart function, blood flow, and blood vessels.
---> Ensure easy navigation and user-friendly interface.
---> Add assessment questions to test understanding.
---> Maintain scientific accuracy according to school-level biology concepts.

FEATURES AND MODULES :

---> Interactive learning
---> Simple explanations
---> Real-life examples
---> Quiz and assessment
---> Visual illustrations and animations
---> Easy navigation

Module 1: Introduction

--> Overview of the Circulatory System.
--> Importance of blood circulation in the human body.

Module 2: Heart

--> Structure of the Heart.
--> Chambers of the Heart.
--> Working of the Heart.
--> Heartbeat and Pulse.

Module 3: Blood Vessels

--> Arteries.
--> Veins.
--> Capillaries.
--> Comparison of different blood vessels.

Module 4: Blood

--> Components of Blood.
--> Red Blood Cells (RBCs).
--> White Blood Cells (WBCs).
--> Platelets.
--> Plasma and its functions.

Module 5: Interactive Simulation

--> Blood flow through the heart and body.
--> Drag-and-drop activities.
--> Identify parts of the heart and blood vessels.
--> Scenario-based learning exercises.

Module 6: Quiz & Assessment

--> Multiple-choice questions.
--> Match the following activities.
--> Score calculation and feedback.

Module 7: Conclusion

--> Summary of key concepts.
--> Learning outcomes.
