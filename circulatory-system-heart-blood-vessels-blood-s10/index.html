Introduction

What is the circulatory system? The circulatory system moves blood, oxygen, and nutrients to every part of the body using the heart and a network of blood vessels.

Why is it important? It delivers oxygen and nutrients to tissues, removes waste, and helps regulate temperature and immunity.

Main Components

Heart

The muscular pump that moves blood through the body.

Blood

Contains red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma.

Blood Vessels

Arteries, veins, and capillaries form the network that carries blood.

Heart Structure and Function

The heart is a muscular pump with four chambers and four major valves that control blood flow.

Four Chambers

  • Right Atrium
  • Right Ventricle
  • Left Atrium
  • Left Ventricle

Four Valves

  • Tricuspid Valve
  • Pulmonary Valve
  • Mitral Valve
  • Aortic Valve

How Blood Circulates

  5. 5Oxygen-rich blood is pumped to the body.

Types of Blood Vessels

Arteries

Carry oxygen-rich blood away from the heart under high pressure.

Veins

Return oxygen-poor blood back to the heart. They have valves to prevent backflow.

Capillaries

Tiny vessels where oxygen and nutrients exchange with tissues.
