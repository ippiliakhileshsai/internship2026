Structure of the Heart

The heart is a muscular organ about the size of a fist. It's divided into four chambers:

  • Right Atrium: Receives oxygen-poor blood from the body
  • Right Ventricle: Pumps blood to the lungs
  • Left Atrium: Receives oxygen-rich blood from the lungs
  • Left Ventricle: Pumps blood to the entire body

Heart Valves

Valves ensure blood flows in one direction:

  • Tricuspid Valve: Between right atrium and ventricle
  • Pulmonary Valve: Between right ventricle and lungs
  • Mitral Valve: Between left atrium and ventricle
  • Aortic Valve: Between left ventricle and body

How the Heart Works

The Heartbeat Cycle:

  1. Diastole: Heart relaxes and fills with blood
  2. Atrial Systole: Atria contract, pushing blood into ventricles
  3. Ventricular Systole: Ventricles contract, pumping blood out

Resting Heart Rate: 60-100 beats per minute

Heart Facts

  • The heart weighs 200-425 grams (7-15 ounces)
  • It's located slightly to the left of center in the chest
  • Protected by the ribcage and pericardium
  • Made mostly of myocardium (heart muscle)

Blood Vessels

The highways of circulation

❤️

Arteries

  • Carry oxygen-rich blood from heart
  • Thick, muscular, elastic walls
  • High pressure blood flow
  • Bright red color
  • Pulse can be felt
💙

Veins

  • Return deoxygenated blood to heart
  • Thin, less muscular walls
  • Low pressure blood flow
  • Dark red/blue color
  • One-way valves prevent backflow
💛

Capillaries

  • Exchange gases and nutrients with cells
  • Microscopic size (7-10 micrometers)
  • Single cell layer walls
  • Extremely permeable
  • Highest surface area for exchange

Blood Components

The precious fluid that sustains life

Red Blood Cells

🔴

Count: 4-6 million/μL

Function: Carry oxygen

Lifespan: 120 days

Color: Hemoglobin (iron)

White Blood Cells

⚪

Count: 4,500-11,000/μL

Function: Protect from infection

Types: Neutrophils, Lymphocytes, Monocytes

Lifespan: Hours to years

Platelets

💜

Count: 150,000-400,000/μL

Function: Form blood clots

Type: Cell fragments

Lifespan: 8-10 days

Plasma

💧

Content: 90% water

Function: Transport medium

Percentage: 55% of blood

Contains: Proteins, hormones, nutrients

Keep Your Heart Healthy

Tips for maintaining a strong circulatory system

🏃

Exercise Regularly

Aim for 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week. Exercise strengthens your heart and improves circulation.

🥗

Eat Healthy Foods

Consume fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid excessive salt and saturated fats.

💧

Drink Water

Stay hydrated by drinking 8-10 glasses of water daily. Proper hydration helps maintain blood volume.

😴

Get Enough Sleep

Sleep 7-9 hours every night. Quality sleep helps regulate blood pressure and heart rate.

🚭

Avoid Smoking

Smoking damages blood vessels and increases heart disease risk. Quitting is the best choice.

🧘

Manage Stress

Practice meditation, yoga, or relaxation techniques. Chronic stress harms cardiovascular health.

Fun Facts

Amazing facts about your circulatory system

1

Heart Beats

Your heart beats approximately 100,000 times per day and pumps roughly 2,000 gallons of blood daily.

2

Blood Volume

Adults have approximately 5 liters of blood (about 10-12 pints) circulating through their bodies.

3

Blood Vessels

If all blood vessels were laid end to end, they would stretch 60,000-100,000 kilometers (37,000-62,000 miles).

4

Continuous Pumping

The heart pumps blood continuously throughout your entire life, 24 hours a day, without ever resting.

5

Blood Pressure

Normal blood pressure is around 120/80 mmHg. The number represents systolic/diastolic pressure.

6

Red Blood Cells

Your body produces approximately 2.4 million red blood cells every second to replace those that die.

7

Pulse Rate

A normal resting heart rate is 60-100 beats per minute, but athletes may have rates as low as 40 bpm.

8

Unique Heartbeat

Your heart's heartbeat signature is as unique as your fingerprints - each person's is completely individual.
