#   Instructions & Requirements

## 1. Light Travels in a Straight Line

### Objective

To observe that light travels in straight lines.

### Apparatus Required

* Torch / Flashlight
* Three cardboard sheets
* Pins or stands
* Dark room

### Procedure

1. Make a small hole at the center of each cardboard sheet.
2. Place the sheets in a straight line.
3. Shine a torch through the holes.
4. Observe the light passing through all three holes.
5. Slightly move one cardboard sheet and observe the result.

### Observation

Light passes only when all holes are aligned.

### Conclusion

Light travels in a straight line.

---

## 2. Formation of Shadows

### Objective

To study how shadows are formed.

### Apparatus Required

* Torch / Lamp
* Opaque object (book, box, cube, etc.)
* White screen or wall

### Procedure

1. Place the opaque object between the light source and screen.
2. Observe the shadow formed on the screen.
3. Move the light source closer and farther from the object.
4. Observe changes in shadow size.

### Observation

The size and shape of the shadow change with the position of the light source.

### Conclusion

A shadow is formed when an opaque object blocks light.

---

## 3. Reflection from a Plane Mirror

### Objective

To verify the law of reflection.

### Apparatus Required

* Plane mirror
* Laser pointer or ray box
* White paper
* Protractor
* Scale
* Pencil

### Procedure

1. Place the mirror vertically on a sheet of paper.
2. Draw a normal line perpendicular to the mirror.
3. Direct a light ray towards the mirror.
4. Trace the incident and reflected rays.
5. Measure the angles using a protractor.

### Observation

Angle of incidence is equal to angle of reflection.

### Conclusion

The law of reflection states:

Angle of Incidence = Angle of Reflection

---

## 4. Rainbow Formation (Dispersion of Light)

### Objective

To observe the dispersion of white light into its constituent colors.

### Apparatus Required

* Glass prism
* White light source (sunlight or torch)
* White screen
* Dark room

### Procedure

1. Allow a narrow beam of white light to pass through the prism.
2. Place a white screen on the opposite side.
3. Observe the colors produced on the screen.
4. Note the order of colors.

### Observation

White light splits into seven colors:
Violet, Indigo, Blue, Green, Yellow, Orange, Red (VIBGYOR).

### Conclusion

White light consists of multiple colors that separate due to dispersion.

---

# Safety Precautions

1. Do not look directly into bright light sources.
2. Never point a laser at a person's eyes.
3. Handle glass prisms and mirrors carefully.
4. Perform optical experiments on a stable surface.
5. Use adult supervision when required.

# Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

* Understand rectilinear propagation of light.
* Explain shadow formation.
* Verify the law of reflection.
* Understand dispersion and rainbow formation.
* Relate simulations to real-world experiments.
