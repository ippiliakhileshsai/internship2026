01 — Foundation

What are Magnets?

Magnets create an invisible region around them where their force can be felt. Every magnet has a north (N) and south (S) pole. Opposite poles attract; like poles repel.

Force: Attraction
N pole → field lines out
S pole → field lines in
02 — Visualisation

Magnetic Field Lines

Field lines show direction and strength of a magnetic field, represented by vector B. Denser lines = stronger field. Lines run from N to S outside the magnet.

03 — Particle Dynamics

Moving Charge in a Field

A charged particle moving through a magnetic field experiences a force perpendicular to both velocity and field. This causes it to curve into a circular path.

06 — Real World

Applications of Magnetism

Magnetic forces power some of the most important technologies in modern life — from music to medicine to transportation.

🔊
Loudspeakers
Current in a coil inside a permanent magnet vibrates a cone, producing sound waves from electrical signals.
⚙️
Electric Motors
Current-carrying coils in a magnetic field experience force — converting electrical energy into rotation.
🚄
Maglev Trains
Superconducting magnets levitate the train above the track, eliminating friction for speeds over 600 km/h.
🏥
MRI Scanners
Powerful fields align hydrogen nuclei in tissue, producing detailed 3D images without radiation.
🧭
Compass
Earth itself acts as a giant magnet. A compass needle aligns with Earth's magnetic field lines.
⚛️
Particle Accelerators
Magnetic fields steer and focus charged particles to near-light speeds in circular tunnels like the LHC.
