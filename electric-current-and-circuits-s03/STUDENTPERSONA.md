## Student Personas

To ensure that the storyboard effectively addresses the learning needs of students from different age groups, learner personas representing Grades 6–10 were developed. These personas helped design content that is engaging, age-appropriate, and educational.


### Grade 6 Students

**Characteristics**
- Age: 11 years
- Prefer visual and story-based learning experiences.
- Enjoy interactive activities and asking questions.

**Challenges**
- Find concepts such as electric current and circuits difficult to understand.
- Learn better through examples than textbook explanations.
- Have limited awareness of electrical hazards in agricultural environments.

**How the Project Addresses These Challenges**
- Introduces concepts through a relatable village-based story.
- Uses simple explanations supported by visuals.
- Connects safety lessons with real-life situations.


### Grade 7 Students

**Characteristics**
- Age: 12 years
- Prefer hands-on and experiential learning.

**Challenges**
- Understand concepts better through demonstrations.
- Find static diagrams difficult to interpret.
- Have limited opportunities for safe experimentation.

**How the Project Addresses These Challenges**
- Incorporates interactive circuit simulations.
- Demonstrates how electricity flows through a complete circuit.
- Encourages exploration in a safe virtual environment.


### Grade 8 Students

**Characteristics**
- Age: 13 years
- Prefer inquiry-based learning and logical explanations.

**Challenges**
- Want to understand the science behind electrical accidents.
- Commonly believe that water itself causes electric shock.
- Struggle to connect theory with practical situations.

**How the Project Addresses These Challenges**
- Explains the role of electrical resistance in the human body.
- Demonstrates how wet skin reduces resistance and increases risk.
- Uses real-world examples involving agricultural water pumps.


### Grade 9 Students

**Characteristics**
- Age: 14 years
- Prefer application-oriented learning.

**Challenges**
- Seek meaningful applications of scientific concepts.
- Find memorization without context unengaging.
- Want to understand how science can solve real-world problems.

**How the Project Addresses These Challenges**
- Links concepts such as current, resistance, and earthing to accident prevention.
- Includes scenarios that encourage critical thinking.
- Demonstrates the importance of science in ensuring community safety.


### Grade 10 Students

**Characteristics**
- Age: 15 years
- Focus on examinations while seeking practical relevance.

**Challenges**
- Prioritize exam preparation over conceptual understanding.
- Often overlook real-world applications of scientific concepts.
- Need effective ways to retain information.

**How the Project Addresses These Challenges**
- Combines concise explanations with visual storytelling.
- Reinforces learning through scenarios and discussions.
- Shows how scientific concepts can prevent accidents and save lives.


### Impact

The student personas guided the development of the storyboard and simulations by ensuring that the content:

- Uses age-appropriate language and examples.
- Addresses common misconceptions about electricity.
- Promotes active learning instead of passive memorization.
- Builds awareness about electrical safety in agricultural settings.
- Encourages learners to apply scientific knowledge to protect themselves and their communities.

By understanding the needs of different learners, this project transforms a complex electrical safety issue into an engaging educational experience that is informative, memorable, and socially impactful.
