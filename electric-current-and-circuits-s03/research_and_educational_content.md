1. Research on Electric Current Concepts

**Electric Current**

Electric current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor such as a metal wire. The movement of electrons creates an electric current. It is measured in amperes (A).

Examples

- Lighting a bulb
- Charging a mobile phone
- Running a fan

Reference: https://www.britannica.com/science/electric-current
---

**Electric Circuit**

An electric circuit is a complete path through which electric current flows from a power source to an electrical device and back.

Components of a Circuit

1. Battery or Cell
2. Connecting Wires
3. Switch
4. Load (Bulb, Fan, Motor)

Types of Circuits

- Open Circuit – Current does not flow.
- Closed Circuit – Current flows normally.
- Short Circuit – Current flows through an unintended path and may cause damage.

Reference: https://www.britannica.com/technology/electric-circuit
---

**Conductors**

Conductors are materials that allow electric current to pass through them easily.

Examples

- Copper
- Aluminum
- Silver
- Iron

Uses

- Electrical wiring
- Power transmission

---

Insulators

Insulators are materials that do not allow electric current to pass through them easily.

Examples

- Rubber
- Plastic
- Glass
- Dry Wood

Uses

- Covering electrical wires
- Safety equipment

Reference : https://www.britannica.com/science/electricity/Conductors-insulators-and-semiconductors
---

Earthing

Earthing is a safety method that provides a low-resistance path for excess electrical current to flow safely into the ground.

Importance of Earthing

- Prevents electric shock
- Protects electrical appliances
- Reduces fire hazards

---

Electrical Safety

Safety Rules

- Never touch electrical appliances with wet hands.
- Do not use damaged wires.
- Avoid overloading electrical sockets.
- Switch off power before repairs.
- Use proper earthing.
- Keep electrical equipment away from water.

2. Real-World Electrical Accident Case Study

Borewell Electric Shock Incident

Incident

A farmer installed an electric motor near a borewell to pump water. Due to damaged insulation and improper earthing, electricity leaked into the metal body of the motor.

A person touched the motor while standing on wet ground and received an electric shock.

Cause

- Damaged wire insulation
- Improper earthing
- Wet surroundings
- Direct contact with the metal body

Scientific Explanation

Electric current always follows a path of least resistance. When the person touched the metal motor, electricity passed through the body to the wet ground, causing an electric shock.

Prevention

- Proper earthing
- Regular maintenance of wires
- Use of insulated cables
- Installation of safety circuit breakers
- Avoid touching electrical equipment with wet hands

3. Educational Explanations for Students

What is Electric Current?

Electric current is the flow of tiny charged particles called electrons through a wire. It helps electrical devices work.

What is a Circuit?

A circuit is a complete path that allows electricity to travel from a power source to a device and back.

What are Conductors?

Conductors are materials that allow electricity to flow easily.

Example: Copper wire.

What are Insulators?

Insulators are materials that block the flow of electricity.

Example: Rubber coating on wires.

Why is Earthing Important?

Earthing protects people and appliances by safely directing extra electricity into the ground.

Why Should We Follow Electrical Safety Rules?

Electrical safety rules help prevent electric shocks, injuries, and accidents.
