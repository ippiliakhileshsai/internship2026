Filtration

Separating solids from liquids using filter paper

📖

📖 Definition

Filtration is a separation method where a mixture of a solid and liquid is passed through a filter (like filter paper) to separate the solid particles from the liquid.

⚙️

⚙️ How Does It Work?

1. The mixture is poured into a funnel lined with filter paper.

2. The liquid (called the filtrate) passes through the tiny holes in the filter paper.

3. The solid particles (called residue) are too big to pass through and remain on the filter paper.

🌍

🌍 Real-Life Examples

☕ Making Tea / Coffee
🚰 Water Purification
🧃 Fruit Juice Straining
🩺 Blood Purification (Kidneys)
🏭 Industrial Water Treatment
🍪 Sieving Flour

💡 Fun Facts

🚰

The water you drink every day is purified using filtration! Water treatment plants use sand and gravel as giant filters.

🫀

Your kidneys filter about 200 liters of blood every single day using a natural filtration process!

☕

Coffee is made by filtering hot water through ground coffee beans – that's filtration in action!
