Distillation

Separating liquids based on their different boiling points

📖

📖 Definition

Distillation is a separation method where a liquid mixture is heated to form vapor, the vapor is then cooled in a condenser, and the pure liquid is collected. It works because different liquids have different boiling points.

⚙️

⚙️ How Does It Work?

1. The liquid mixture is heated in a distillation flask.

2. The component with the lowest boiling point evaporates first and rises as vapor.

3. The vapor passes through a condenser where it is cooled by cold water and turns back into liquid.

4. The purified liquid is collected in a separate flask.

🌍

🌍 Real-Life Examples

⛽ Crude Oil Refining (Petrol/Diesel)
🚢 Ships Making Drinking Water
🌹 Perfume & Essential Oils
🥃 Alcohol Distillation
🌊 Desalination Plants
🔬 Laboratory Water Purification

💡 Fun Facts

⛽

Petrol, diesel, kerosene, and LPG are all separated from crude oil using a process called fractional distillation!

🚢

Pure drinking water can be made by distilling sea water — ships and submarines use this method!

🌸

The perfume industry uses distillation to extract fragrant oils from flowers and plants!
