Plastics: Types, Properties and Environmental Issues
-

Introduction:
-
 Plastics are synthetic or semi-synthetic materials made from polymers. 
 The word polymer means a substance composed of many repeating units called monomers. 
 Plastics have become an essential part of modern life because they are lightweight, durable, inexpensive, and easy to manufacture

Types of Plastics:
-

Plastics are mainly classified into two categories: 

 1.Thermoplastics and 

2.Thermosetting Plastics.

1. Thermoplastics
   -

Thermoplastics are plastics that can be softened by heating and hardened by cooling repeatedly without undergoing any chemical change. 
They can be recycled and reshaped into new products.

 Examples:
 Polyethylene (PE),
 Polypropylene (PP),
 Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC),
 Polystyrene (PS)

 Applications:
  Plastic bags,
  Water bottles,
  Food containers,
  Pipes and fittings,
  Packaging materials,

2. Thermosetting Plastics
   -
Thermosetting plastics become permanently hard after being heated once. 
They cannot be remelted or reshaped because a permanent chemical structure is formed during the heating process.

  Example :
  -
  Bakelite,
  Melamine

 Applications:
 -
  Electrical switches,
  Kitchen utensils,
  Electronic circuit boards,
  Heat-resistant products,

 Properties of Plastics:
 -
  1.Lightweight
 
  2.Durable
 
  3.Water Resistance,
 
  4.Corrosion Resistance 
 
  5.Electrical Insulation
 
  6.Easy Mouldability
 
 
Environmental Issues Caused by Plastics:
-

 1.Non-Biodegradable Nature

 2.Soil Pollution

 3.Water Pollution

 4.Harm to Marine Life

 5.Air Pollution

 6.Microplastic Pollution

 7.Impact on Wildlife

Solutions to Plastic Pollution:
-

 Several measures can help reduce the environmental impact of plastics.
 -

Reduce:
Minimize the use of single-use plastics such as bags, straws, and disposable containers.

Reuse:
Use plastic products multiple times whenever possible.

Recycle:
Separate plastic waste and send it for recycling to reduce landfill accumulation.
