🏏 The 12th Man – Equality, Prejudice & Discrimination

---

📌 Project Title

The 12th Man: Understanding Equality, Prejudice & Discrimination in Cricket Team Selection

---

📖 Description of the Project

The 12th Man is an educational web-based simulation game designed to teach the concepts of equality, prejudice, and discrimination through a cricket team selection scenario.

The game places the user in the role of a player attempting to earn a place in the team. Through different stages of the selection process, users observe how unfair decisions based on prejudice can exclude deserving players and negatively affect team performance.

By allowing users to make their own team selections and observe the outcomes, the project promotes fairness, inclusion, and merit-based decision-making.

---

❗ Problem Statement

In many teams and organizations, decisions are sometimes influenced by prejudice, favoritism, stereotypes, or discrimination instead of actual performance and ability.

As a result:

- Talented individuals may lose opportunities.
- Team performance may decrease.
- Confidence and motivation can be affected.
- Equality and inclusion are compromised.

The challenge is to ensure that every individual receives a fair opportunity based on skills, effort, and performance rather than personal bias.

---

💡 Solution

This project provides an interactive simulation that helps users:

- Understand the concepts of equality, prejudice, and discrimination.
- Observe the effects of biased decision-making.
- Experience fair and unfair team selection outcomes.
- Learn why merit-based selection improves team success.
- Promote awareness about fairness and inclusion.

🧮 Algorithm

Team Selection Algorithm

1. Load player data.
2. Calculate total score for each player.
3. Sort players based on performance.
4. Select the top-performing players.
5. Compare user selection with the ideal team.
6. Determine whether the selection is fair.
7. Simulate match outcome.
8. Display analysis and rewards.
